What Makes Us Different

When it comes to weight management it's easy to get confused, focusing on the amount of weight lost versus new habits getting formed-results vs process.

What’s often forgotten is that weight loss is an outcome of the changes that we make.

Research shows that the best predictor of sustainable weight loss is the new habits we build-not how much weight we have lost that day, week, or even that month.

In fact, evidence shows that the best predictor of weight loss is NOT the diet you choose to follow (e.g. keto, low carb, vegan, etc.) but the way in which you implement the changes.

Unfortunately, results are rarely linear; the change we start today rarely gives us the immediate feedback we're looking for to know if we're on the right track tomorrow.



That's why it's crucial to learn how to navigate and evaluate the process. It gives you the confidence that, even if changes don't immediately take place, you'll eventually get there if you stick with things.

How To Become A Dietitian

Earning the title of Registered Dietitian is hard. You need to:

- Go to University for at least four years
- Study everything from biochemistry to physiology to nutrition
- Pass both Provincial and National  exams administered by a regulated licensing body

However, for the team at Constant Health, that’s the equivalent of lacing up your shoes: the training has just begun.

If it was as simple as eat less do more, 64% of Canadians wouldn’t be classified as having excess weight. 

Weight is influenced by our environment, genes, hormones, medications, behaviour, and other medical conditions. Themes that are touched upon in school, but certainly are not the focus.

How To Become A Constant Health Dietitian

At Constant Health our dietitians aren’t just required to delve deeper into the medical science of weight management. They also learn the behavior change skills we know are necessary to make permanent lifestyle changes.

Training for Constant Health’s RDs and health coaches includes:

- Twice monthly workshops with our Clinical Psychologist in sessions designed to learn how to utilize CBT (Cognitive Behavior Therapy) in supporting behaviour change.

- Weekly peer supervision, as well as monthly journal and book clubs to ensure that they’re up to date on the latest evidence around weight management and behavior change.
